Chicken Pot Pie

Description

This chicken pot pie is my husband's favorite dinner. It's full of chicken, creamy sauce, and a smattering of vegetables-- all wrapped in a homemade crust.

This is a perfect recipe to make ahead, as it freezes and reheats beautifully. Serve with salad, rolls, or both! Serves 8.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • One recipe Double Crust
  • 3 cups Cooked cubed chicken
  • Half a potato, peeled and diced to 1/4"
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1/2 cup all purpose flour
  • One medium onion, 1/4" diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Herbs
    • 1/2t dried thyme
    • 1/2t dried oregano
    • 1/2t umami blend (optional--adds depth)
  • 2-3 cups chicken stock
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Make double crust and refrigerate. Preheat oven to 375F.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onions and herbs and cook the onions until translucent.
  3. Add the garlic and cook for another minute, until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the flour and cook for a minute or two, until sizzling.
  5. Add the chicken stock, whisking constantly until the mixture boils and thickens (about 3-5 minutes). Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Add potatoes and let simmer for 10 minutes or until potatoes have just started to soften.
  7. Add chicken, peas and carrots. Cook for another minute until warmed through.
  8. Remove the crust from the refrigerator and roll one half to 1/8-1/4" thickness. Set into the pie plate.
  9. Pour the filling into the pie crust. You do not need to prebake the crust.
  10. Roll out the top crust and place on top of the pie. Trim and crimp the edges. Cut several decorative slits in the top crust to vent steam.
  11. Place the pie on a rimmed baking sheet and bake on the bottom rack of the oven for one hour or until the bottom crust has turned golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  12. Let cool for a few minutes before serving. Or, let cool completely, refrigerate or freeze, and reheat to serve later.
